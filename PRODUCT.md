# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Nuvela serves small-to-mid-sized teams coordinating project work across multiple people. The primary user is a project manager who owns delivery, creates projects, assigns work, reviews progress, and needs a reliable view of what is moving, blocked, overdue, or ready for completion. Secondary users are collaborators who need a focused view of assigned work, a simple way to move tasks forward, and enough context to discuss work without noise. Owners and Admins handle organization setup, account creation, access, and offboarding.

Users are usually inside an operational workflow rather than browsing. They need clarity, predictable controls, and trustworthy feedback more than novelty.

## Product Purpose

Nuvela is a calm, multi-tenant task-management workspace where organizations plan projects, move work across a board, and stay in sync through reliable real-time notifications. It exists to prevent small teams from losing ownership, duplicating tasks, or missing stalled work.

Success means each role can complete its core work without permission confusion: Owners can create an organization, Admins can manage users, Project Managers can own and review projects, and Collaborators can focus on their assigned tasks. The interface should make responsibility obvious, keep notifications meaningful, and preserve a clear audit trail of important task activity.

## Brand Personality

Calm, focused, and quietly premium.

Nuvela should feel restrained and deliberate, closer to Linear, Height, Things, Notion, and Vercel than to a busy enterprise dashboard. The product should earn trust through consistency, spacing, contrast, and clear hierarchy rather than decoration.

## Anti-references

Nuvela should not look like a generic AI-purple SaaS dashboard, a loud enterprise admin panel, a saturated Kanban toy, or a dense cockpit. Avoid decorative gradients, glassmorphism, heavy shadows, noisy notification treatments, full-color overload, and inconsistent component shapes. The interface should not train users to ignore alerts by making everything feel urgent.

## Design Principles

1. Calm comes from restraint. Use neutral surfaces, subtle borders, generous spacing, and scarce accent color.
2. Ownership must be visible. Role boundaries, project ownership, assignment, review gates, and archived/read-only states should be obvious in the UI.
3. Signal beats noise. Notifications and status treatments should draw attention only when a user needs to act.
4. Familiar controls win. The product is a task tool; standard buttons, forms, menus, tables, panels, and board interactions should feel predictable.
5. One system, many roles. Owner, Admin, Project Manager, and Collaborator variants should share the same visual vocabulary while exposing only the right actions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Nuvela targets WCAG AA contrast for all meaningful text and controls. Color must never be the only signal: statuses, priorities, destructive actions, and locked states need labels or icons as well as color. Focus states must remain visible. Body text should stay at 14px or larger, with 12px reserved for non-essential metadata. Motion should be limited to state feedback and should respect reduced-motion preferences.
